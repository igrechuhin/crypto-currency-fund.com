The Crypto Currency Fund (CCF) is the world’s first crypto currency trading fund. It employs a variety of strategies in trading crypto currencies to maximize return and minimize risk, including event-driven, arbitrage (timing, exchange, inter-crypto currency, etc.), cyclic and volatility. As CCF and crypto currency markets evolve, the strategies will become more numerous and sophisticated and become more extensive, adding shorts (currently possible, but with very limited liquidity), swaps, other combinations of instruments, etc. Its initial focus is almost exclusively on bitcoin, but it is expanding slowly to include ”altcoins” (non-bitcoin crypto currencies) as they develop sufficient liquidity.

 

Because crypto currency exchanges work 24/7, the Fund’s traders have to monitor markets around the clock. To do so, Crypto Currency Fund has traders located in New York, London and Moscow. Over time, the number of traders will expand as the strategies and altcoins are refined and as the Fund grows in size.

 

Unlike several other funds in the crypto currency space, which do not provide discretionary management, invest only in bitcoin and passively buy and sell bitcoin with their investors’ money as those investors complete subscription and redemptions into and out of the fund, CCF actively trades in bitcoin, litecoin and, eventually, other altcoins.

 

In doing so, CCF’s traders employ three strategies: buy-and-hold, event driven and volatility-based. At its creation, most of the Fund was invested in the first strategy and all of it was is invested in Bitcoin. This allocation is changing slowly and, ultimately, the buy-and-hold strategy will represent the smallest allocation – as, otherwise, CCF and its traders obviously add no value. CCF’s allocation is also shifting from 100% bitcoin to adding altcoin investments, although, unless one or more other crypto currencies displace bitcoin as the dominant player in that space, the allocation will remain mainly in bitcoin.
